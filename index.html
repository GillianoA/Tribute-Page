Elon Musk

Innovative entrepreneur, visionary leader, and technological disruptor

Elon Musk, originally from Pretoria, South Africa, embarked on a journey that took him from humble beginnings to become a global business magnate and influential figure in the tech industry.

Here's a time line of Elon Musk's life:

  • 1971: Elon Musk is born on June 28th in Pretoria, South Africa.
  • 1988: Moves to Canada to attend Queen's University, but leaves after two years to pursue studies in the United States.
  • 1995: Drops out of Stanford University to co-found Zip2, a software company.
  • 1999: Compaq acquires Zip2 for $307 million, providing Musk with his first significant financial success.
  • 2002: Co-founds SpaceX (Space Exploration Technologies Corp.), with the goal of making space travel more affordable and accessible.
  • 2004: Co-founds Tesla Motors (now Tesla, Inc.), an electric car manufacturer, with the mission to accelerate the transition to sustainable transportation.
  • 2006: Helps create SolarCity (now Tesla Energy), a solar energy services company.
  • 2008: Suffers setbacks with SpaceX as three consecutive rocket launches fail, putting the company at risk of bankruptcy.
  • 2008: Achieves success with SpaceX's fourth attempt at launching the Falcon 1 rocket, becoming the first privately funded company to send a liquid-fueled rocket into orbit.
  • 2010: Tesla Motors goes public, becoming the first American car company to do so since Ford in 1956.
  • 2012: SpaceX becomes the first privately-funded company to send a spacecraft (Dragon) to the International Space Station.
  • 2015: Unveils the Tesla Model X, an all-electric SUV.
  • 2017: Launches SpaceX's Falcon Heavy rocket, which becomes the most powerful operational rocket in the world and successfully sends a Tesla Roadster into space.
  • 2018: Founds The Boring Company, focused on tunneling and infrastructure technology.
  • 2020: Overtakes Jeff Bezos to become the world's richest person, according to Forbes' Real-Time Billionaires List (though the title fluctuates).
  • 2021: Tesla becomes the world's most valuable automaker by market capitalization, surpassing Toyota.
  • 2021: SpaceX launches the first all-civilian mission to orbit (Inspiration4), marking a significant milestone in commercial space exploration.
